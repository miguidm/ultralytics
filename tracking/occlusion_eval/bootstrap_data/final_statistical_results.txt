================================================================================
 METHODOLOGY: HOW SIGNIFICANCE TESTING WAS CONDUCTED
================================================================================
1. Bootstrapping (Variance Generation):
   ANOVA mathematically requires variance (multiple replicates per model) to calculate
   significance. To avoid the computationally prohibitive cost of retraining all 9 YOLO
   models 5 separate times from scratch, we employed Bootstrapping on the evaluation set.
   We randomly sub-sampled 800 images from the 1,624-image OccludedYOLO dataset 5 distinct
   times (creating 5 Bootstrap IDs). Every model was evaluated on these exact same 5 subsets.
   This yielded 5 mAP50-95 and 5 Recall scores per model (N=5), establishing the required
   variance for ANOVA while isolating the architectural performance from dataset bias.

2. Testing Framework:
   - Part 1 (One-Way ANOVA): Tests if applying ANY modification is generally better than none.
   - Part 2 (Two-Way ANOVA): A fully-crossed factorial analysis on the 8 DCN variants to
     isolate the main effects and interaction of DCN Version (v2 vs v3) and Placement 
     (Backbone, FPN, PAN, Full). Vanilla is excluded to prevent matrix rank deficiency (NaN).
   - Part 3 (Tukey HSD Post-Hoc): Directly compares every specific modified model against the
     Vanilla baseline to see exactly WHICH modifications achieved statistical significance.

ACADEMIC INTERPRETATION FOR PART 2 (TWO-WAY ANOVA):
To isolate the architectural mechanisms driving performance under occlusion, a Two-Way ANOVA
was conducted exclusively on the deformable models, analyzing the main effects of DCN Version
(v2 vs. v3) and Placement (Backbone, Neck-FPN, Neck-PAN, Neck-Full). The results indicated a
highly significant main effect for both Version (p < 0.001) and Placement (p < 0.001) on mAP50-95.
Furthermore, a highly significant interaction effect was observed between the version and its
placement (p < 0.001), demonstrating that the benefits of DCNv3 are maximized only when integrated
into specific Neck layers, rather than universally applied across the network.
===================

===================
ACADEMIC INTERPRETATION FOR PART 3 (POST-HOC):
To evaluate the overall impact of structural modifications against the baseline, a post-hoc
analysis using Tukey's Honestly Significant Difference (HSD) test was conducted. The analysis
demonstrated that modifications applied specifically to the Neck architecture using DCNv3
(e.g., DCNv3n-FPN, DCNv3n-PAN) provided highly significant gains (p < 0.001) over the Vanilla
baseline. Conversely, modifications restricted solely to the Backbone (DCNv2n-Liu, DCNv3n-Liu)
or over-parameterized networks (DCNv3n-Full) did not achieve statistical significance,
confirming that the spatial location of the deformable convolution is critical for occlusion robustness.
